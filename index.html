Does Synthetic Layered Design Data Benefit Layered Design Decomposition?

Abstract

Recent advances in text-to-image generation have signifi- cantly accelerated progress in autonomous graphic design generation. However, a gap remains between visually plausible generation and indus- trial usability. A key missing capability is layer decomposition: practical design workflows require separating an image into structured compo- nents, typically foreground, background, and text layers. Unfortunately, high-quality layer-annotated data are largely proprietary assets in the industry, and publicly available datasets remain extremely limited. Mo- tivated by this observation, we investigate whether scalable synthetic data can serve as an effective substitute for private layered design re- sources. We curate structured layer-level annotations and construct a controllable data generation pipeline based on the open-source CLD framework. Using this organized synthetic layer data, we train models for layout and layer extraction. Surprisingly, models trained primarily on synthetic layered data achieve strong performance and generalize well to real-world graphic designs, suggesting that realistic layer supervision may not require large-scale proprietary datasets. Our findings indicate that properly structured synthetic data can be a viable path toward scalable layer understanding. To facilitate future research, we commit to releasing the dataset construction pipeline to the community, aiming to promote progress toward practical, editable text-to-image systems.

METHOD

Our framework utilizes a main backbone and a parallel control module for precise layer decomposition.
